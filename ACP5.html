Facts about Horses

Horses are large, powerful mammals that have been domesticated by humans for thousands of years for transportation, work, and companionship. Belonging to the species Equus ferus caballus, horses are herbivores with a strong digestive system adapted to grazing. They typically live 25 to 30 years and come in various breeds, each suited to specific tasks like racing, farming, or riding. Known for their speed, strength, and intelligence, horses communicate through vocalizations, body language, and ear movements. They have excellent memory and can form strong bonds with humans, making them valuable in therapy and sports as well.
